Protect and Restore Forests

We work to reduce deforestation, promote reforestation, and protect the world's vital forest ecosystems.
Tree planting and reforestation helps to reduce climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. Trees would absorb and store carbon in their trunks, branches, and roots, which helps reduce greenhouse gases.

Forests also improves air quality, preventing soil erosion, and regulates the water cycle. They also provide habitats for many wildlife species, which also helps to restore biodiversity. Not only it benefits for us human beings, but also for wildlife.

In cities, planting trees can reduce temperature by cooling the surrounding environments, and can also reduce the emission of carbon dioxide near industrial areas. Because of these environmental benefits, tree planting is considered one of the most important and effective solutions in preventing climate change.
