Why is This Happening?

Burning fossil fuels When we burn fossil fuels like coal and gas to create electricity or power our cars, we release carbon dioxide pollution into the atmosphere.
Deforestation Humans clear vast areas of vegetation worldwide for agriculture, urban and infrastructure development, or to sell products such as timber and palm oil.
Agriculture The greenhouse gases emitted from agriculture are methane, nitrous oxide and carbon dioxide. Livestock like cattle and sheep produce a large amount of methane.
Transportation Greenhouse gases are emitted from burning fossil fuels for cars, trucks, ships, trains and planes.
Industry manufacturing In the manufacturing industry, energy is needed to create steel, cement, aluminium, chemicals, food processing and many other materials, such as paper, that we use every day.

What Will Happen?

Hotter temperatures As greenhouse gas concentrations rise, so does the global surface temperature. The last decade, 2015-2024, is the warmest on record.
Rising sea levels Increased ocean temperatures are melting glaciers and ice caps worldwide, causing rising sea levels and threatening low-lying islands and coastal cities.
Intense extreme weather events Extreme weather events like bushfires, cyclones, droughts and floods are becoming more frequent and intense due to global warming.
Warming oceans Warming waters are bleaching coral reefs and driving stronger storms. Rising ocean acidity threatens tiny crustacean populations that would disrupt the entire marine food chain.
